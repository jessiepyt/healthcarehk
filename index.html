The slow growth rate of public hospital beds over the past decade results in today health care crisis

Hundreds of Hong Kong medical staffs protested against overcrowding and lack of resources at public hospitals earlier this year.This story looks inside the statistics of hospital beds to understand what results in today’s overcrowding problem.

Why it matters:

  • According to the Food and Health Bureau ,Hong Kong public hospitals are serving 90 percent of the patients.
  • There’s an increasing demand for medical services caused by the city’s growth of the aging population. According to the Census and Statistics Department , Hong Kong population aged 65 and over is projected to increase from 11.7% in 2003 to 27% in 2033. The health care crisis will worsen if not handled properly by now.

By the numbers:

  • The overall increase rate of hospital beds in the past decade is slow, 4.5% only.
  • The growth rate of hospital beds for general over the past decade is also slow, 1.8% only.
  • There are no changes in the total number of hospital beds for the infirmary of all hospital clusters between 2008-2016, except for 2017. However, that’s because three hospitals were re-delineated from the Kowloon West Cluster to Kowloon Central Cluster in 2017.
  • There’s an absence of hospital beds for mentally handicapped in Hong Kong East Cluster, Hong Kong West Cluster, Kowloon Central Cluster, Kowloon East Cluster, and New Territories East Cluster over the past decade. There’s no increase in hospital beds for mentally handicapped in Kowloon West cluster and New Territories West cluster over between 2008 - 2015 and only 20 more new beds are added in New Territories West cluster between 2016 - 2017.
  • There’s no increase in the total number of hospital beds for the mentally ill in the past decade.

What they’re saying:

  • The Secretary for Food and Health Sophia Chan said last year that the Hospital Authority has already provided 250, 231 and 229 new beds in 2015, 2016, 2017 respectively and would open a total of 574 additional beds in 2018-19.
  • Legislator Kwok Ka-ki criticized the government lack of long term and holistic public health care planning earlier this year. He said that the government uses shortages of doctors as an excuse to not increase the number of hospital beds.

What’s next:

  • Hong Kong Cheif Secretary Matthew Cheung said last month that 5,000 more hospital beds would be added by 2026 under the 10-year Hospital Development Plan.He urged the legislators to pass the funding for the plan.
